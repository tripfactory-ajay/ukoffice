UK–India Liaison & Contracting Workflow

1

UK Office Identifies Contracting Requirements

The UK Office, led by the Commercial Team, identifies the need for new hotel product and contracting opportunities across each destination. This is driven by market demand, customer booking patterns, seasonal trends, and feedback from sales channels.

The UK Commercial Team works closely with Destination Managers in India to review existing hotel inventory and identify any gaps — including destinations with high demand but limited contracted supply, or areas requiring additional hotel categories for different customer segments such as budget, mid-range, or premium.

Through regular communication and market analysis, the UK Office provides clear guidance to the India team regarding priority destinations, target hotels, and the type of product required. This ensures contracting efforts are aligned with commercial strategy.

2

UK Office and India Destination Manager Collaboration

Following identification of contracting requirements, the UK Commercial Team works collaboratively with the Destination Manager in India to refine product needs for each destination — combining the UK Office's market demand knowledge with the local destination expertise held by the India team.

The Destination Manager provides guidance on which hotel packages and product types are required to be commercially successful, advises on realistic and competitive price ranges, and shares competitor research including links to comparable packages currently sold by other operators.

This allows the UK Office to benchmark pricing, understand competitor positioning, and ensure that contracted hotel rates are negotiated at the correct levels to create attractive and competitive packages.

3

UK Office Contract Negotiation and Supplier Agreements

The UK Office, together with the wider European Commercial Team, takes responsibility for negotiating and securing commercial agreements with suppliers across Europe — including hotels, DMCs, transportation providers, and other relevant service partners.

The UK team approaches selected suppliers and begins formal discussions around rates, availability, contract terms, and value-added services. The objective is to secure competitive pricing structures that allow the business to create attractive packages while maintaining appropriate commercial margins.

All supplier agreements must meet operational and quality standards required for the UK and European markets, including contract terms, cancellation policies, seasonal rate structures, and allocation agreements.

4

India Destination Manager Product Execution and Sales Management

Once supplier contracts are finalised by the UK Office, the Destination Manager in India takes responsibility for executing the product within operational systems — loading contracted hotels, services, and package components into the relevant booking platforms and ensuring all product information is accurate and ready for sale.

The India team manages day-to-day operational aspects including maintaining availability, updating pricing within the agreed contract structure, and ensuring packages remain competitive in the market. The Destination Manager also oversees the presentation of product to ensure hotel descriptions, images, and package details are correctly displayed.

As sales activity develops, the India team monitors booking performance and provides regular reporting back to the UK Office covering package performance, customer demand patterns, pricing competitiveness, and any supplier issues that arise.
